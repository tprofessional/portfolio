Trisha Moorkoth

My passion for computer science began in the summer of 2015, as a soon-to-be sixth grader. I had attended a two-week-long camp that introduced me to programming in C as well as web development. Since then, I have been working towards using my problem solving skills to develop code in order to solve problems or complete tasks that are important and useful to me. I aim to be a professional software developer or networks analyst in the future, so I try to get as much hands-on experience as I can by joining clubs, competing in CyberPatriot, taking online courses, and finding events like Hackathons and cyber Security Capture the Flag challenges to participate in. I enjoy programming because I am able to apply both my logical and creative thinking into developing code and implementing it into situations where I am solving a practical problem.

My experiences with cyber security and developing programs have taught me the importance of collaboration, patience, and persistence. Being on a CyberPatriot team has helped me realize the importance of practice when becoming well-versed in a subject. When I first started, I did not have the slightest understanding of why it was necessary to secure digital devices, but after reviewing information I found online, attending college courses, and using practice images, I was able to easily complete the challenges I was faced with during the competitions. I learned that cooperation and discussion was important for us to progress with any ideas that we had in order to further develop them into something tangible and practical. As it was my first experience with cyber security, there were many instances in which I felt confused and did not know what I was supposed to be doing. To overcome this, I asked for help from my mentors and teammates, who were always willing to work together to resolve any issues that came up. Currently, my team and I are competing in CyberPatriot's Platinum Tier, an accomplishment I am very proud of when I look back at where I started.

From these experiences, I learned valuable collaborative skills such as effective communication and the ability to ask for help when I need it. I apply the lessons I have learned from facing challenges to my daily life, because patience, practice, and the drive to learn are what allowed me to discover my passion for computer science and cyber security.
