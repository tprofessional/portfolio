Online Portfolio

Trisha Moorkoth

My passion for computer science began in the summer of 2015, as a soon-to-be sixth grader. I had attended a two-week-long camp that introduced me to programming- specifically in C- and web development. Since then, I have been working towards using my problem solving skills to develop code in order to solve problems or complete tasks that are important and useful to me. I aim to be a professional software developer or networks analyst in the future by taking part in activities, clubs, and other events that I can use and learn programming in with peers who share similar interests as I do. The reason why I have such an affinity for programming and learning about digital networks is because I am able to apply both my logical and creative thinking into developing code and implementing it into situations where I am solving a practical problem, or simply entertaining my imagination.

Throughout my experiences in working with code and developing web apps, I have noticed that collaboration, patience, and persistence are all essential skills to have. The first website I ever contributed to developing was a project integrated into the summer camp that introduced me to the world of computer science. I had to work with three other peers of varying experience to create a website for a fake business of our choice. I learned that cooperation and discussion was important for us to progress with any ideas that we had and how to further develop them into something tangible and practical in regards to real-world problems. As it was my first experience with code, there were many instances in which I felt confused and did not know what I was supposed to be doing. To overcome this, I asked for help from my mentors and teammates, who were always willing to work together to fix any issues that had come up. In this way, patience, along with persistence, was crucial in developing the code that would be used in our final product.

By joining clubs, I was able to hone skills that would helped me develop myself as a person, as well as a programmer. The most helpful of these were the Fallon Engineering Lab (FEL) that I was accepted to in sixth grade, and the cyber security club I founded in eighth grade. In joining FEL, I built and programmed my first robot with guidance from coaches and mentors and the assistance of my peers. From these experiences, I learned leadership skills- public speaking, communication, etc.- as well as practical skills such as collaboration and organization, which I find to be very useful not only when generating code, but also in my daily life.

All in all, I have gained many skills from my experiences with computer science and engineering- whether it be programming, learning cyber security, or building- that I continue to use in everything that I do. Thank you for your time, please feel free to explore the rest of my portfolio.
