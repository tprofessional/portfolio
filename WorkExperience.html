Introduction: Career Exploration

During my time in the engineering academy, I have met with industry professionals through workplace field trips and guest speakers. Connecting with these industry professionals has helped me narrow my focus on the career path I am most interested in. Hearing about their work, internship, and college experience is helpful because I learn the most efficient and useful ways to apply for programs and courses that are relevant to my ideal career and collegiate experiences.

Guest Speaker: Maynard Holiday
Year: 2019

Maynard Holiday is a Senior Tech Advisor to the US government and he told us about his work in infrastructure. One of the projects he worked on was working on the Chernobyl reactors after it exploded in 1986. To observe the aftermath without exposing oneself to the high levels of radiation at the site, Maynard described how scientists used IBM robots to take video footage of the area and would analyze it remotely.

Guest Speaker: Jason Yosinski
Year: 2019

Being interested in artificial intelligence, one of the guest speaker presentations I found most interesting was a presentation by Jason Yosinski, founder of Uber AI Labs. During this presentation, I learned more about the concept of machine learning and how machine learning programs “write themselves” after conducting multiple trials. Since this presentation, I have searched for opportunities and workshops to learn more about AI.

Guest Speakers: Lars Borg, Tom Shefler
Year: 2020

During this presentation, Lars Borg and Tom Shefler covered the topics of how the Moon was formed, as well as what kind of methods of dating were used to determine the evolution of the Earth and Moon after their separation. I found this lecture interesting and relevant because it connects to what we have learned in class about the design process, specifically about constructing and testing a prototype. In the case of determining the age of the Moon, the first model used to do so can be considered a prototype, and the testing of this prototype was using a modified method of using half-lives to redate the rocks.

Guest Speakers: Dr. Matt Coleman, Dr. Mattias Frank, Dr. David Loftus, Erin M. McKay
Year: 2020

In this talk, Dr. Coleman, Dr. Frank, Dr. Loftus, and Ms. McKay addressed medical concerns with sending astronauts to Mars. These included the amount of time and distance needed to travel to Mars, how to stay healthy on the way to and on Mars, and what kind of technology would be most suited for assessing the health of astronauts traveling in deep space. Emphasis was placed on how each team member working to produce the medical device had to communicate clearly and effectively with others because of the nature of the product and because teams were made up of professionals in different fields (chemistry, biology, engineering). This talk gave me insight into the development process of medical devices, but not just those that would be used normally, but ones that would have to function outside the Earth's atmosphere.

Roche Job Shadow
Year: 2020

I recently participated in a job-shadow field trip to Roche labs. During this job-shadow, I toured the different facilities used for each type of job at Roche, gained advice from mentors, and discovered how the work environment at Roche is maintained. The most valuable experience during this field trip was being able to talk with employees at Roche about essential skills needed for their work. During the Q&A session with mentors, I was able to talk to Dr. Persis Wadia, who works in Research and Development. She spoke about the importance of writing precise and thorough documentation for diagnostic products being developed. I found this skill to be relevant to my own career goals because, with my experiences in courses including Computer Science Principles and Principles of Engineering, I realize that documentation is an important part of every engineering project. Additionally, I met Dr. Iqbal Zahidi, Director of Operations at Roche. I gained valuable advice about what to put on a resume when applying for a job, and that a resume should showcase what you can provide for the company you apply to.
